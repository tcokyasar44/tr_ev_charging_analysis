\section*{Abstract}
\vspace{10pt}
\noindent\textbf{Objectives}\\
As electric vehicle (EV) adoption grows, quantifying the scheduling burden and economic cost of long-distance travel under existing charging infrastructure is crucial. This paper evaluates how well current infrastructure serves long-distance trips and determines the travel cost premium relative to internal combustion engine (ICE) vehicles. It addresses the need to jointly optimize per-vehicle charging schedules across hundreds of thousands of vehicles simultaneously.\\

\noindent\textbf{Methods}\\
We develop a scalable, optimization-based framework for scheduling EV charging stops along long-distance trajectories across the United States. The methodology uses three phases: infeasibility pruning, per-vehicle optimal charging schedule computation via dynamic programming on a directed acyclic graph, and capacity-aware iterative congestion resolution using a penalty-based heuristic. The framework is applied to approximately 2.7 million origin-destination trajectories from a 1\% sample of national travel demand in POLARIS, covering 14,260 Direct Current Fast Charging (DCFC) stations.\\

\noindent\textbf{Findings}\\
The framework yields capacity-feasible schedules in under 1.3 hours on a 128-core cluster without commercial solvers. A case study comparing DCFC and Level 2 networks reveals that feasibility depends on network geography, not charger power. Additionally, 350 kW ultra-fast charging achieves total cost of ownership parity with ICE vehicles for 86\% of feasible paths.\\

\noindent\textbf{Novelty}\\
This study introduces the first national-scale scheduling framework optimizing en-route charging for millions of vehicles simultaneously, resolving capacity conflicts without commercial solvers.\\

\noindent\textbf{Practical Applications}\\
Results show charger power upgrades should target high-utilization stations to resolve congestion rather than uniform deployment. This framework provides policymakers a diagnostic tool to assess how EV range, adoption rates, and energy prices impact long-distance EV travel viability.
